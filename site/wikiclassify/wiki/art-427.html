rural nobility He had two brothers, Francesco Montini, who became a physician, and Lodovico Montini, who became a lawyer and politician {Sfn| Lazzarini|1964|pp=20–21}} On 30 September 1897, he was ''[[Arnaldo da Brescia]]'', a public school in [[Brescia]] His education was often interrupted by bouts of illness n 1916, he entered the [[seminary]] to become a Roman Catholic priest He was ordained priest on 29 May 1920 in Brescia and celebrated his first [[Holy Mass]] in Brescia in the [[Santa Maria delle Grazie, Brescia|Basilica of Santa Maria delle Grazie]] {Sfn|Lazzarini|1964|p=26}} Montini Secretariat of State in 1922, where he worked under Pizzardo together with [[Francesco Borgongini-Duca]], [[Alfredo Ottaviani]], [[Carlo Grano]], [[Domenico Tardini]] and [[Francis Spellman]] {Sfn|Lazzarini|1964|p=31}} Consequently, he spent not a day as a parish priest In 1925 he helped found the publishing house Morcelliana in Brescia, focused on promoting a 'Christian inspired culture'
