(October 31, 1887&nbsp;– April 5, 1975) was a Chinese political and military leader who served as the [[President of the Republic of China|leader of the Republic of China]] between 1928 and 1975 He is known as '''Chiang Chung-cheng''' (蔣中正, Jiang Zhongzheng) or '''Chiang Chieh-shih''' (蔣介石, Jiang Jieshi) in [[Standard Chinese]] Chiang was an influential member of the [[Kuomintang]] (KMT), the Chinese Nationalist Party, and was a close ally of [[Sun Yat-sen]] He became the [[Commandant]] of the Kuomintang's [[Whampoa Military Academy]] and took Sun's place as leader of the KMT when Sun died in 1925 In 1926, Chiang led the [[Northern Expedition (1926–1927)|Northern Expedition]] to unify the country, becoming China's nominal leader lt;ref name=a>{{Cite book|last=Zarrow|first=Peter Gue|year=2005|title=China in War and Revolution, 1895–1949|pages=230–231}}</ref> He served as Chairman of the [[National Military Council]] of the Nationalist government of the [[Republic of China (1912–1949)|Republic of China]] (ROC) from 1928 to 1948 Chiang led China in the [[Second Sino-Japanese War]] (the Chinese theater of [[World War II]]), consolidating power from the party's [[Warlord Era|former regional warlords]] Unlike Sun Yat-sen, Chiang Kai-shek was socially conservative, promoting traditional Chinese culture in the [[New Life Movement]] and rejecting western democracy and the [[Socialist ideology of the Kuomintang|nationalist democratic socialism]] that Sun embraced in favour of an authoritarian government hiang's predecessor, Sun Yat-sen, was well-liked and respected by the Communists, but after Sun's death Chiang was not able to maintain good relations with the [[Chinese Communist Party]] (CCP) [[Shanghai massacre of 1927|A major split]] between the Nationalists and Communists occurred in 1927; and, under Chiang's leadership, the Nationalists fought a nationwide [[Chinese Civil War|civil war]] against the Communists After [[Second Sino-Japanese War|Japan invaded China in 1937]], Chiang agreed to a temporary truce with the CCP Despite some [[Battle of Taierzhuang|early cooperative military successes against Japan]], by the time that the Japanese surrendered in 1945 neither the CCP nor the KMT trusted or was actively cooperating with the other fter the [[Marshall Mission]], an American-sponsored attempt to negotiate a coalition government, failed in 1946, the Chinese Civil War resumed The CCP defeated the Nationalists in 1949 [[Odd Arne Westad|Westad]] says the Communists won the Civil War because they made fewer military mistakes than Chiang Kai-Shek,
