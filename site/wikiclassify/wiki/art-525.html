English portrait and landscape painter, draughtsman, and printmaker He surpassed his rival [[Sir Joshua Reynolds]] to become the dominant British [[portrait]]ist of the second half of the 18th century He painted quickly, and the works of his maturity are characterised by a light palette and easy strokes He preferred landscapes to portraits, and is credited (with [[Richard Wilson (painter)|Richard Wilson]]) as the originator of the 18th-century British landscape school Gainsborough was a founding member of the [[Royal Academy of Arts|Royal Academy]] ==Youth and training==[[File:Thomas curiosities lt;ref>Fulcher, George William, ''Life of Thomas Gainsborough'', London 1856</ref> The artist spent his childhood at what is now [[Gainsborough's house|Gainsborough's House]], on
