referred to as illuminated printing, and the finished products as illuminated books or prints Illuminated printing involved writing the text of the poems on copper plates with pens and brushes, using the design standing in relief (hence the name) his is a reversal of the usual method of etching<!--same article as relief etching-->, where the lines of the design are exposed to the acid, and the plate printed by the [[Intaglio (printmaking)|intaglio]] method Relief etching (which Blake referred to as "[[Stereotype (printing)|stereotype]]" in ''The Ghost of Abel'') was intended as a means for producing his illuminated books more quickly than via intaglio Stereotype, a process invented in 1725, consisted of making a metal cast from a wood engraving, but Blake's innovation was, as described above, very different The pages printed from these plates were hand-coloured in water colours and stitched together to form a volume Blake used illuminated printing for most of his well-known works,
