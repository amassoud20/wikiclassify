to take solo parts in the church when he was ten years of age Important products of this period are six ''sonate a quattro'', or string sonatas, composed in three days, unusually scored for two violins, cello and double bass The original scores, dating from 1804, when the composer was twelve, were found in the [[Library of Congress]] in Washington D Often transcribed for string orchestra, these sonatas reveal the young composer's affinity for [[Joseph Haydn|Haydn]] and [[Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart|Mozart]], already showing signs of operatic tendencies, punctuated by frequent rhythmic boy in pieces Though it was Rossini's first opera, written when he was thirteen or fourteen, the work was not staged until the composer was twenty years old, premiering as his sixth official opera n 1806, Rossini became a [[cello]] student under Cavedagni at the Conservatorio di Bologna The following year he was admitted to the counterpoint class of Padre [[Stanislao Mattei]] (1750–1825) He learned to play the cello with ease, but the pedantic severity of Mattei's views on counterpoint served only to drive the young composer's views toward a freer school of composition His insight into orchestral resources is generally ascribed not to the strict compositional rules that he learned from Mattei, but to knowledge gained independently while scoring the quartets and symphonies of Haydn
