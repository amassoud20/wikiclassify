{{post-nominals|country=GBR|sep=,|KG|GCB|GCH|PC|FRS}} (1 May 1769 – 14 September 1852), was an [[Anglo-Irish people|Anglo-Irish]] soldier and statesman, and one of the leading military and political [[British Army]] in 1787, serving in Ireland as [[aide-de-camp]] to two successive [[Lord Lieutenant of Ireland|Lords Lieutenant of Ireland]] He was also elected as a [[Member of Parliament]] in the [[Irish House of Commons]] He was a colonel by 1796, and saw action in the [[Flanders Campaign|Netherlands]] and in India, where he fought in the [[Fourth Anglo-Mysore War]] at the [[Siege of Seringapatam (1799)|Battle of Seringapatam]] He was appointed governor of [[Seringapatam]] and [[Mysore]] in 1799 and, as a newly appointed major-general, won a decisive victory over the [[Maratha 1814, he served as the [[List of ambassadors of the United Kingdom to France|ambassador to France]] and was granted a dukedom During the [[Hundred Days]] in 1815, he commanded the [[Seventh Coalition|allied]] army which defeated Napoleon at Waterloo, together with a [[Prussian Army|Prussian army]] under [[Gebhard Leberecht von Blücher|Blücher]] Wellesley's [[Battle record of Arthur style of warfare, resulting in several victories against a numerically superior force while minimising his own losses He is regarded as one of the greatest defensive commanders of all time, and many
