details" lt;ref>Bacigalupo (1999), 188–191</ref> The [[leitmotif]]s in Pound's literary criticism are recurrent patterns found in historical events, which, he believed, through the use of judicious Reading]]'', he sought to emphasize the value of art and to "aestheticize the political"&nbsp;– written forcefully, according to Nadel, and in a "determined voice" lt;ref>Nadel (1999), 10</ref> In form his criticism and essays are direct, repetitive and reductionist, his rhetoric minimalist, filled with "strident impatience", according to Pound scholar Jason Coats, and frequently failing to
