{{postnominals|country=GBR|GCB|KCMG|PC|PCc|QC}} (11 January 1815&nbsp;– 6 June 1891) was a Canadian politician and Father of Confederation who was the [[List of Prime Ministers of Canada|first Prime fought to block provincial efforts to take power back from Ottawa His most controversial move was to approve the execution of Métis leader [[Louis Riel]] for treason in 1885; it permanently alienated the Francophones who saw themselves humiliated Macdonald was born in Scotland; when he was a boy his family immigrated to [[Kingston, Ontario|Kingston]] in the colony of [[Upper Canada]] (today in
