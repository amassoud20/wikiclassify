sound of his primary instrument, the [[pipe organ]], ''i ', alternating between two groups of instruments, as when changing from one manual of the organ to another === Structure ====The structure of Bruckner's symphonies is in a way an extension of that of [[Beethoven]]'s symphonies Bruckner's symphonies are in four movements '''The first movement''', in [[Time signature|4/4]] or [[Time signature|2/2]], is, from [[Symphony No 2 (Bruckner)|Symphony No 2]] on, an [[Allegro (music)|allegro]] in modified [[sonata form]] with three thematic groups The first group is mostly displayed in [[Dynamics (music)#Relative loudness|piano]] or [[Dynamics (music)#Relative loudness|pianissimo]] on a [[tremolo]] of the string instruments and is, after a long [[crescendo]], repeated in [[tutti]] The second group, melodious and in A-B-A’ [[lied]] form, is mostly of [[Contrapuntal motion|contrapuntal structure]] The third group, mostly [[rhythm]]ical and often in [[unison]], is sometimes a variant of the first group, as in [[Symphony No 4 (Bruckner)|Symphony No 4]] The often extensive [[Musical development|development]] is followed by a modified and somewhat shortened [[reprise]] and a powerful [[Coda (music)|coda]] '''The second movement''', mostly an [[Adagio (music)|adagio]] in 4/4, is generally in ABA’B’A’’ lied form The first thematic group, sometime rhythmical, is developed and magnified in the second and third parts The second group is mostly a [[melody]] in cantilena form The adagio is put in third position in the first version of [[Symphony No 2 (Bruckner)|Symphony No 2]], and in [[Symphony No 8 (Bruckner)|Symphony No 8]] and [[Symphony No 9 (Bruckner)|Symphony No 9]] '''The [[scherzo]]''' in [[Time signature|3/4]] and in [[minor mode]] is often fiery The, sometimes very short, trio is more melodious and often in [[Ländler]] form The ''[[da capo]]'' reprise is, in Bruckner's early symphonies, ending with a short, powerful coda The revised version of the [[Symphony No 4 (Bruckner)|Symphony No 4]] features a scherzo – the "Hunt scherzo" – in which the outer sections are in 2/4 and in [[major mode]] '''The [[Finale (music)|Finale]]''', in 4/4 or 2/2, is, as the first movement, an allegro in modified sonata form with three thematic groups The first group, often a kind of [[Introduction (music)|introduction]], is followed by a second, melodious and often contrapuntal group, and a third, rhythmical and often in unison, group, which is sometimes a variant of the first group, as in [[Symphony No 2 (Bruckner)|Symphony No 2]] The development, often of dramatic character, is followed by a less formal reprise, which is sometimes inverted (C’-B’-A’) as in [[Symphony No 7 (Bruckner)|Symphony No 7]], and a coda in which the first thematic group of
