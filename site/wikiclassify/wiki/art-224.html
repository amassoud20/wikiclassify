"The Holy Spirit is truly Spirit, coming forth from the Father indeed but not after the manner of the Son, for it is not by generation but by ''procession'', since I must coin a word for the sake of clearness quot;<ref>Gregory of Nazianzus, ''Five Theological Orations'', oration five This fifth oration deals entirely with the Holy Spirit lt;/ref> Although Gregory does not fully develop the concept, the idea of procession would shape most later thought about the Holy Spirit lt;ref>HEW Turner and Francis Young, "Procession(s)" in ''The Westminster Dictionary of Christian Theology'', ed A between Eastern and Western Christianity lt;/ref>He emphasized that Jesus did not cease to be God when he became a man, nor did he lose any of his divine attributes when he took on human nature in the [[Old Testament]] but much clearer since the ascension of Jesus into Heaven and the descent of the Holy Spirit at the feast of Pentecost n contrast to the Neo-Arian belief that the Son is ''anomoios'', or "unlike" the Father, and with the [[Semi-Arian]] assertion that the Son is ''[[homoiousios]]'', or "like" the Father, Gregory and his fellow Cappadocians maintained the Nicaean
