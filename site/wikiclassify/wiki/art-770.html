vacations to these activities lt;ref>Asratyan, p 8</ref> Although able to read by the age of 7, Pavlov was seriously injured when he fell from a high wall onto stone pavement;<ref>Asratyan, p 9</ref> he did not undergo formal schooling until he was 11 years old as a result of his injuries lt;ref name="Credo Reference"/>Pavlov attended and graduated from the Ryazan church school before entering the local theological seminary However, in 1870, Pavlov left the seminary without graduating to attend the university at St Petersburg where he enrolled in the physics and math department and took natural his research work After a competitive examination, Pavlov won a fellowship at the Academy for postgraduate work lt;ref>Asratyan, p 13</ref> The fellowship and his position as Director of the Physiological Laboratory at the clinic of the famous Russian clinician, [[Sergey Botkin|S P Botkin]] enabled Pavlov to continue his research work In 1883, he presented his doctor's thesis on the subject of ''The centrifugal nerves of the heart'' and posited the idea of ''nervism'' and the basic principles on the trophic function of the nervous system Additionally, his collaboration with the
